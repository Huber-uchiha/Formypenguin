Family

When it comes to my family, I want to be brutally honest—no filters, no pretending. I love them unconditionally, but it’s not some perfect, fairytale kind of love. They’re my family, and while I’m grateful for everything they’ve given me, the emotional bond isn’t always simple or deep in the way people might expect. That said, I know I’m lucky in a lot of ways. I’ve had support, opportunities, and basic needs met—and for that, I’ll always carry this silent promise in my heart to repay everything they've done for me, in every possible way I can. Let me start with my dad. My relationship with him is... complicated, to say the least. It’s kind of a rollercoaster. Some days, it feels like he loves me. Other days, it feels like he resents me—or I resent him. It’s mutual, honestly. I respect and even love him for the good things he’s done for me, the times he’s shown up. But at the same time, I struggle with how he treats my mom and sometimes even me. It’s like we’re just tolerating each other, using each other to survive. He looks at me as his ticket to a better future, especially as he gets older. And I’m not gonna lie—I lean on him too. He supports my expenses and basic needs, so yeah, it’s a weird dynamic. That’s one big reason why I’ve always dreamed of being the best dad ever someday. I want my future kid—especially if it’s a boy—to feel the kind of love I never fully got. Not just words, but actual love. Real support, understanding, time, warmth. I want to be a safe space for my child, not a source of confusion or fear. Now, about my mom—I’ve got a lot of mixed feelings there too. I love her, I respect her, and I appreciate everything she’s done for us. She’s strong in her own way, but I hate seeing her take the side of someone who doesn’t treat her well. Sometimes I get mad, thinking “Why is she still putting up with this?” But deep down, I understand. Maybe she stays because she feels like there’s no other option—especially with us kids depending on her. That hurts to accept, but I get it. Still, I don’t talk to my mom or dad much. Not because I don’t care, but because I just can’t handle the emotional weight of those relationships all the time. Then there are my sisters. I’m not super emotionally attached to them, but they’ve always shown me love in their own way. And that doesn’t go unnoticed. I really want to repay that love by being there for them—especially the younger ones. The youngest one, man... she’s got attitude and ego for days, lol. A whole queen in her own mind. 👑 And the middle one? She’s kinda scary when she’s mad—not gonna lie. Push her too far and she might just throw hands. But still, I’ve got love for both of them. And I’ve got more sisters beyond them, and I treat them all with the same respect and care. I know I’m not the perfect son or brother. Honestly, I feel like I’m just figuring it all out as I go, carrying emotional weight I don’t always talk about. But what I do know is this: one day, I want to build a future that’s different from the one I came from. A future full of love, peace, and respect—the kind I crave now. And I’m willing to work hard, learn, grow, and hustle until I get there. That’s my truth—for now, at least.
